Hikes, coffee, sights, and some of Seattle's best food — curated for a late March long weekend while you have a few hours to yourself.

☁️ Late March: pack layers, expect some rain, embrace it

Discovery Park Loop

🥾 Hike

📍 Magnolia, Seattle

Seattle's largest park — forest trails wind down to a historic lighthouse and a secluded pebble beach on Puget Sound. You'll forget you're in a city within five minutes. Multiple loops from 2 to 5 miles.

Lincoln Park

🥾 Hike

📍 West Seattle · 8011 Fauntleroy Way SW

A West Seattle gem with 5 miles of forested trails opening onto views of the Olympics and Puget Sound. A long coastal trail runs right along the waterline. Free parking, no fees. Great on a clear day — or a moody rainy one.

✓ March-safeIn-cityEasy–Mod
Maps ↗

Seward Park

🥾 Hike

📍 Rainier Valley · 5900 Lake Washington Blvd S

A peninsula of old-growth forest on Lake Washington — there's reportedly a 500-year-old tree in there. Easy 2.5-mile loop with constant lake views and Mt. Rainier on clear days. Peaceful, beautiful, and rarely crowded.

✓ March-safeIn-cityEasy
Maps ↗

Washington Park Arboretum

🥾 Hike

📍 Madison Park · 2300 Arboretum Dr E

A 230-acre living tree museum managed by the UW. Late March is special — early camellias and magnolias are blooming. Boardwalk paths, lily pad ponds, sequoia groves. Free and open daily 8am–8pm.

✓ March-safeIn-city🌸 Blooms
Maps ↗

Twin Falls

🥾 Hike

📍 North Bend · ~40 min east

Mossy forest, river views the whole way, and a gorgeous multi-tiered waterfall at the end. Easy and well-maintained. In late March the falls run strong from snowmelt — exactly when this hike is at its best.

✓ March-safeDay tripEasy
Maps ↗

Franklin Falls

🥾 Hike

📍 Snoqualmie Pass · ~50 min east

One mile each way, minimal elevation gain, jaw-dropping waterfall at the end. Very family-friendly. Note: check trailhead access in March — use Exit 52 if Exit 47 is gated.

✓ March-safeDay tripEasy
Maps ↗

Rattlesnake Ledge

🥾 Hike

📍 North Bend · ~35 min east

Seattle's most popular day hike: a steady 4-mile round-trip through forest opens onto a dramatic rocky ledge with sweeping views of Rattlesnake Lake. Go on a weekday if you can — weekends fill up fast.

✓ March-safeDay tripModerate
Maps ↗

Wallace Falls

🥾 Hike

📍 Gold Bar · ~1 hr north

Multiple waterfall viewpoints through lush PNW forest. Spring is peak season — the falls are thunderous from snowmelt. About 5.5 miles round-trip with some elevation gain.

✓ March-safeDay tripModerate

Espresso Vivace

☕ Coffee

📍 Capitol Hill · 532 Broadway E

A Seattle institution since 1988. Pioneered Italian-style espresso craft in the US. Order the Nico or a velvet mocha. Sidewalk bar available for coffee on the go. Open from 6am daily.

ClassicLatte art

Ghost Note Coffee

☕ Coffee

📍 Capitol Hill · 1623 Bellevue Ave

Drinks made with cocktail shakers and house ingredients. The Lush Life (espresso, orange blossom honey, almond milk) is floral and perfect. The espresso float — iced latte with local root beer — is the one to try.

Craft drinksInventive

Victrola Coffee Roasters

☕ Coffee

📍 Capitol Hill · 310 E Pike St

A roastery and café in a 1920s building on Pike Street. Single-origin focus, on-site cupping room, always buzzing. They taste hundreds of cups a week to maintain their standard — it shows. Good seating.

Single-originGood seating

Lighthouse Roasters

☕ Coffee

📍 Fremont · 400 N 43rd St

30+ years in a 120-year-old Craftsman house in upper Fremont. Deliberately no WiFi — their sign reads "we prefer a café full of conversation." One of Seattle's best neighborhood institutions. Pair with a walk through Fremont.

Neighborhood gemNo WiFi

QED Coffee

☕ Coffee

📍 West Seattle · 6080 35th Ave SW

A cozy, plant-filled neighborhood café with great burritos and inventive drinks — the iced coffee with ginger honey syrup is a standout. Open 6am weekdays, 7am weekends. Perfect before or after Lincoln Park.

West SeattleFood too

Mr. West Cafe Bar

☕ Coffee

📍 Downtown · 720 Olive Way

Cozy modern café-bar downtown with serious espresso and great food. The cortado with cardamom syrup is exceptional, the Turkish eggs are a hit, and the Seattle Fog is a local favorite. Good for a sit-down breakfast before a day out.

Post Alley Pizza

🍽️ Kenji Pick

📍 Downtown · 1123 Post Ave (near Pike Place)

A tiny walk-up slice shop tucked just off the waterfront. No seating — just perfect pizza to eat while you wander. Daily specials, meat and veggie. Walk here after Pike Place Market.

"Makes quite possibly the best slices in town."— Kenji López-Alt

Temple Pastries

🍽️ Kenji Pick

📍 Central District · 2524 S Jackson St

Kenji's go-to for croissants. Beautiful pastry case, great coffee, plant-filled interior. Get there before 9:30am on weekends for the best selection. The gruyère and shallot croissant is especially good.

"The king of croissant makers."— Kenji López-Alt via Apple Maps

Rachel's Bagels & Burritos

🍽️ Kenji Pick

📍 Ballard · 5451 Leary Ave NW

Kenji's shoutout caused such demand the shop had to double production — "The Kenji Effect" was born here. The Nick & Nora with lox is the move. Closed Tuesdays. Open 8am–1pm, so go early.

14 Carrot Cafe

🍽️ Kenji Pick

📍 Eastlake · 2305 Eastlake Ave E

A funky beloved diner open for breakfast and lunch. Kenji is a regular. The carrot cake pancakes with cream cheese are the reason to come. Crab cake Benedict also excellent. Closed Tuesdays. Open 8am–2pm.

"Custardy brioche griddled golden brown with soft butter — exactly what French toast should be."— Kenji López-Alt

Aviv Hummus Bar

🍽️ Kenji Pick

📍 Capitol Hill · 107 15th Ave E

Fresh-made falafel, hot puffy pita, silky hummus, and spicy condiments. Israeli counter food at its best. Order the falafel plate with fried eggplant and zhug. Closed Mondays.

"Everything I want in a falafel bar."— Kenji López-Alt

Kilig

🍽️ Kenji Pick

📍 International District · 710 8th Ave S

Filipino comfort food with precision. Kenji raves about the sizzling sisig (pork shoulder, runny egg) and the honey-chili chicken wings with an impossibly crisp crust. Open Wed–Sun from noon.

"Filipino comfort food with precision and heart."— Kenji López-Alt

Loretta's Northwesterner

🍽️ Kenji Pick

📍 South Seattle · 8617 14th Ave S

A no-nonsense South Seattle dive bar that takes its food seriously. Tavern burger, fish and chips, and pork cutlet sandwich — Kenji says all three are in contention for best in the city. Dark, relaxed, great burgers. 21+.

"Not just one, but three items would be high in the running for best in the city."— Kenji López-Alt via The Infatuation

Buckshot Honey

🍽️ Kenji Pick

📍 Snoqualmie · 40 min east

A cozy BBQ spot right in Snoqualmie — perfect after Twin Falls or Rattlesnake Ledge. The smoked brisket on rye with roasted onions and pickled peppers is the move. Also: cheddar apple pie and braised collards.

"A perfect post-hike stop. Don't sleep on the braised collards or the cheddar apple pie."— Kenji López-Alt

Proper Fish

🍽️ Kenji Pick

📍 Bainbridge Island · ferry ride

British-born chef Harvey Wolff makes fish and chips worth the 35-minute ferry crossing. Huge fillet, golden batter, thick-cut fries, fresh mushy peas. The ferry over offers beautiful skyline views heading out of downtown.

"The best fish and chips around. Worth the ferry ride."— Kenji López-Alt

Pike Place Market

🌆 Sight

📍 Downtown Waterfront

Fish-throwing, flower vendors, great produce, and the original Starbucks (skip the line). Best on a weekday morning before 10am when you can actually move around. Post Alley Pizza and Mr. West are both nearby.

IconicMorning

The Gum Wall

🌆 Sight

📍 Post Alley · 1428 Post Alley (under Pike Place)

A brick alley plastered floor-to-ceiling in chewed gum — one of Seattle's most delightfully weird attractions. Open 24 hours. Add your own piece. Smells like spearmint and chaos. Worth the five minutes, right after or before Pike Place Market.

FreeWeird + funQuick stop
Maps ↗

Olympic Sculpture Park

🌆 Sight

📍 Belltown · 2901 Western Ave

A free, outdoor SAM-run sculpture park on the waterfront with sweeping views of Elliott Bay, the Olympics, and the Seattle skyline. Walking paths wind through large-scale artwork down to the shoreline. All-weather, always open, always free.

FreeWaterfrontAll-weather

Seattle Japanese Garden

🌆 Sight

📍 Arboretum · 1075 E Lake Washington Blvd

A meticulously designed traditional Japanese garden tucked inside the Arboretum — koi pond, maple groves, stepping stones, and a tea house. Small and serene. Late March often brings early cherry blossoms. Closed Mondays. Small admission fee.

Small fee🌸 BloomsTue–Sun

Chihuly Garden and Glass

🌆 Sight

📍 Seattle Center · 305 Harrison St

Extraordinary glass sculpture gardens next to the Space Needle. Especially stunning on a grey day when colors pop against the sky. The Glasshouse has a massive suspended installation — one of the most photogenic spots in Seattle.

PaidAll-weather

Kerry Park

🌆 Sight

📍 Queen Anne · 211 W Highland Dr

The classic Seattle skyline view — Space Needle, downtown, Elliott Bay, and on clear days Mt. Rainier framed behind everything. Best at golden hour or after dark. Free and quick. Find street parking one block away.

FreeViewsQuick stop

Fremont Neighborhood

🌆 Sight

📍 Fremont

Seattle's self-proclaimed "Center of the Universe." The giant troll sculpture under the Aurora Bridge, the year-round Sunday market, and Lighthouse Roasters all within walking distance. Quirky, walkable, full of character.

Capitol Hill

🌆 Sight

📍 Capitol Hill

Seattle's best walking neighborhood. Dense with great coffee (Espresso Vivace, Victrola, Ghost Note all within blocks), good food (Aviv, Kilig), independent bookshops, and street life. Just wander.

Perfect day combos

Ideas for your free time

→West Seattle Day: QED Coffee → Lincoln Park coastal trail → Loretta's Northwesterner for lunch. All in one neighborhood.
→In-City Nature: Arboretum walk (spring blooms!) + Japanese Garden → Seward Park loop by Lake Washington → 14 Carrot for brunch if you time it right.
→Waterfall + BBQ: Morning drive to Twin Falls → lunch at Buckshot Honey in Snoqualmie on the way home.
→Capitol Hill Morning: Temple Pastries → Espresso Vivace or Ghost Note → Aviv for lunch.
→Ferry Adventure: Downtown ferry to Bainbridge Island (35 min, skyline views both ways) → lunch at Proper Fish → ferry back. A perfect half-day.
→Classic Downtown: Mr. West for breakfast → Pike Place Market + Gum Wall → Post Alley Pizza for lunch → Olympic Sculpture Park → Kerry Park for the view.
